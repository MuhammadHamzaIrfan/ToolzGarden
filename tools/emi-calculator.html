What Is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment — the fixed amount you pay to your lender every month until your loan is fully repaid. Each EMI payment consists of two parts: a portion that reduces your outstanding principal, and a portion that covers the interest charged for that month. In the early months of a loan, a larger share of your EMI goes toward interest. As you repay, more of each payment goes toward the principal — this is called loan amortization.

Understanding your EMI before taking a loan helps you plan your monthly budget, compare lenders, and choose the right loan tenure for your financial situation.

EMI Formula Explained

EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)^n] ÷ [(1 + r)^n − 1]

Where: P = Principal loan amount  |  r = Monthly interest rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)  |  n = Loan tenure in months

Example: Loan of ₹5,00,000 at 8.5% for 5 years (60 months): r = 8.5/12/100 = 0.00708. EMI ≈ ₹10,235 per month. Total payment ≈ ₹6,14,115. Total interest ≈ ₹1,14,115.

Reducing Balance vs Flat Rate EMI

Most banks in India use the reducing balance method (also called diminishing balance), where interest is calculated on the outstanding principal each month. As you repay, the principal reduces, so you pay less interest over time.

Some lenders (especially informal ones) use the flat rate method, where interest is calculated on the original loan amount throughout the tenure. The flat rate method results in significantly higher total interest paid. This calculator uses the standard reducing balance method used by all major banks and financial institutions.

Tips to Reduce Your EMI

  • Make a larger down payment — Reduces the principal, directly lowering the EMI
  • Negotiate a lower interest rate — Even 0.5% less can save thousands over the loan period
  • Extend the tenure — Lowers EMI but increases total interest paid
  • Improve your credit score — A CIBIL score above 750 typically unlocks lower rates
  • Make partial prepayments — Paying extra toward principal reduces outstanding balance faster
