Understanding Your Results

Your Monthly Installment (EMI) is the fixed amount payable each month. It covers both principal repayment and interest. The Total Interest shows the true cost of borrowing — the shorter the term, the less interest you pay overall.

The EMI Formula

EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)^n] ÷ [(1 + r)^n − 1]

Where P = Principal, r = Monthly rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = Number of months.

Tips to Reduce Your Loan Cost

  • Choose the shortest term you can comfortably afford — it saves significant interest.
  • Improve your credit score before applying to unlock lower rates.
  • Compare multiple lenders — even 0.5% difference matters over years.
  • Make lump-sum prepayments to reduce the outstanding principal faster.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the monthly loan installment calculated?
EMI = [P × r × (1+r)^n] ÷ [(1+r)^n − 1], where P is principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the number of monthly payments.
Is this loan calculator free?
Yes, completely free. No account, no signup, no payment required. Everything runs in your browser — your data never leaves your device.
Does a longer loan term reduce my monthly payment?
Yes — a longer term lowers your monthly EMI but significantly increases the total interest paid. Use the calculator to compare different terms.
Can I use this for a mortgage or auto loan?
Yes. This calculator works for any fixed-rate loan — personal loans, auto loans, home loans, and education loans. Actual mortgage costs may include taxes and insurance not captured here.
What is loan amortization?
Amortization is the process of paying off a loan through scheduled payments. Early payments have more interest; later payments have more principal. Toggle the repayment schedule above to see month-by-month.
What is the difference between EMI and installment?
EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) and installment refer to the same thing — the fixed monthly payment to repay a loan. EMI is the common term in South Asia; "monthly payment" or "installment" is standard elsewhere.
