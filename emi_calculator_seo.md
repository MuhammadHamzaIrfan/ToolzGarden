## 3.4 H2: What Is EMI?

An **Equated Monthly Installment (EMI)** is a fixed payment made by a borrower to a lender on a specific date every calendar month. EMIs are used to repay both the **principal** (the amount originally borrowed) and the **interest** (the lender's charge for providing credit) over the agreed loan tenure.

EMIs are structured so that the total repayment is spread evenly across months, making financial planning straightforward. In the early months of a loan, a larger portion of your EMI goes toward paying interest. As the loan matures, progressively more of your payment reduces the principal — this is the foundation of the **reducing balance method**, the standard used by banks globally.

For example, if you borrow ₹5,00,000 at 10% per annum for 5 years (60 months), your EMI would be approximately ₹10,624 per month. Over the entire tenure, you'd pay ₹6,37,480 in total — meaning ₹1,37,480 is the total interest cost.

**Key takeaway:** A lower interest rate and shorter tenure means a lower total interest outflow, even if the monthly EMI is slightly higher.

## 3.8 H2: EMI Formula Explained

The standard EMI formula used by all banks and financial institutions is:

```
EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)^n] / [(1 + r)^n − 1]
```

Where:
- **P** = Principal loan amount
- **r** = Monthly interest rate = Annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100
- **n** = Loan tenure in months
- **^** = Exponent (power)

### Worked Example

**Loan:** ₹5,00,000 | **Rate:** 10% per annum | **Tenure:** 5 years (60 months)

1. r = 10 / 12 / 100 = **0.008333**
2. (1 + r)^n = (1.008333)^60 = **1.6453**
3. Numerator = 5,00,000 × 0.008333 × 1.6453 = **6,855.48**
4. Denominator = 1.6453 − 1 = **0.6453**
5. EMI = 6,855.48 / 0.6453 = **₹10,624 per month**

**Total Payment** = ₹10,624 × 60 = ₹6,37,440
**Total Interest** = ₹6,37,440 − ₹5,00,000 = **₹1,37,440**

> **Special case:** If the interest rate is 0%, EMI = P / n (simple division), since the standard formula produces a division-by-zero error. Our calculator handles this automatically.

## 3.9 H2: Reducing Balance vs Flat Rate EMI

Understanding this distinction can save you significant money when comparing loan offers.

### Reducing Balance Method (Standard / Correct)
In this method — used by all RBI-regulated banks — **interest is calculated on the outstanding principal** each month. As you repay the principal, the interest component shrinks and the principal component grows. This is the method used in this calculator.

**Example:** For a ₹1,00,000 loan at 12% for 1 year:
- EMI = ₹8,885/month
- Total Interest = ₹6,620

### Flat Rate Method (Common in Dealer Financing)
In this method, **interest is calculated on the original principal** for the entire tenure, regardless of how much you've repaid. This results in a significantly higher effective interest cost.

**Example:** For the same ₹1,00,000 loan at 12% flat rate for 1 year:
- EMI = ₹9,000/month
- Total Interest = ₹8,000

| Comparison | Reducing Balance | Flat Rate |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Interest basis | Outstanding principal | Original principal |
| Effective rate (12% flat ≈) | ~21–22% effective | 12% nominal |
| Total interest (₹1L, 1yr) | ₹6,620 | ₹8,000 |
| Used by | All banks (RBI mandate) | Some dealers, NBFCs |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always ask your lender whether they quote flat rate or reducing balance. A 12% flat rate is equivalent to approximately 21% reducing balance — almost double!

## 3.10 H2: Tips to Reduce Your EMI

1. **Make a Larger Down Payment:** The less you borrow, the lower your EMI. Even a 10% larger down payment can meaningfully reduce your monthly burden.
2. **Choose a Longer Tenure:** Spreading the loan over more months lowers your EMI — but increases total interest paid. Balance carefully.
3. **Negotiate a Lower Interest Rate:** A difference of even 0.5% per annum can save tens of thousands over a long-tenure loan.
4. **Make Prepayments:** Extra payments directly reduce your outstanding principal, lowering future interest. Most home loans allow partial prepayment without penalty.
5. **Transfer to a Lower-Rate Lender:** If rates drop significantly, consider a balance transfer to refinance at a better rate.
6. **Improve Your Credit Score:** Borrowers with CIBIL scores above 750 often qualify for lower interest rates, directly reducing EMI.
7. **Avoid Processing Fees That Inflate Cost:** Some lenders roll fees into the loan amount, increasing the base on which EMI is calculated.

### Q4: What is the formula used to calculate EMI?
The calculator uses the standard **reducing balance EMI formula**:
`EMI = [P × r × (1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1]`
where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n = tenure in months. This is the same formula used by all RBI-regulated banks in India.

### Q5: Can I calculate EMI for a 0% interest loan?
**Yes.** When the interest rate is set to 0%, the calculator automatically switches to a simple division formula: EMI = Principal ÷ Tenure (months). This avoids a division-by-zero error while giving you the correct result.

### Q7: What is an amortization schedule?
An amortization schedule is a complete month-by-month table showing how much of each EMI payment goes toward principal repayment and how much goes toward interest, along with the remaining outstanding balance. It helps you visualize your loan's progress over time.

function calculateEMI(principal, annualRate, tenureMonths) {
  if (annualRate === 0) {
    return principal / tenureMonths;
  }
  const r = annualRate / 12 / 100;
  const n = tenureMonths;
  const emi = (principal * r * Math.pow(1 + r, n)) / (Math.pow(1 + r, n) - 1);
  return emi;
}

Derived values:
  totalPayment = emi * tenureMonths
  totalInterest = totalPayment - principal
  principalPercent = (principal / totalPayment) * 100
  interestPercent = (totalInterest / totalPayment) * 100

Amortization schedule per month:
  balance = principal
  for each month 1..n:
    interestComponent = balance * r
    principalComponent = emi - interestComponent
    balance -= principalComponent (cap at 0 for rounding)
    row = { month, principalComponent, interestComponent, emi, balance }
